How it works

A rule is three parts. AI is one of them.

Build it in the rule builder — no code. The engine watches for the event, checks your condition, and only then runs the action. That's why the outcome is predictable every time.

01 — Trigger

Something changes

A status change, a shift in completeness score, or a product matching a saved query. The rule listens for the event and reacts to it — continuously.

02 — Action

The engine runs the action

Set or correct an attribute, change status, sync to channels — or run AI Enrich and AI Linguist. One rule, many possible actions, on the model and channels you've connected.

03 — Result

Validated, then applied

Output is checked against your data model and either applied automatically or routed to a person for review. Broken rules are detected and deactivated on their own.

Control

The rule fires. You stay accountable.

Automation shouldn't mean giving up the parts you're answerable for. Control is built into the mechanism, not bolted on after.

  • Every rule is readable. Trigger and condition are stated in plain terms — anyone can see why it ran.
  • Review or auto-apply, per rule. Keep a person in the loop where judgment matters; automate the rest.
  • Output is validated. AI results are checked against your data model before anything is written.
  • Self-healing. Rules that break are detected and deactivated automatically — no silent misfires.
