Why this isn't the AI you already have

Suggesting isn't doing.

Most PIMs now have AI that drafts, flags or proposes — and a human still clicks. The work is faster, but it's still being done one click at a time.

What you have today
AI that suggests
Drafts, flags, proposes. A person still clicks.
What agentic means
Agents that act
Onboard, enrich, validate, publish. The work gets done.

Real automation means the work runs without anyone steering it through — agents that act, not features that suggest.

Here's the work that now handles itself:

Supplier onboarding

Feeds in any format — PDF, CSV, EDI — ingested and mapped automatically. Or suppliers contribute through a self-serve portal, with AI doing the mapping alongside them.

Enrichment

Attributes filled, descriptions drafted, gaps closed — continuously, across every market.

Validation

Checked against your rules as it happens, not in a periodic QA cycle after the fact.

Publishing

Channel-ready output, shaped per channel, in sub-minute time — not a release project.

Agents drive, humans govern

Speed, without losing control.

The worry with automation is handing over a process you're still accountable for. So control is built into how the agents work — not bolted on afterward.

  • Every action an agent takes is logged, validated and traceable
  • The rules stay yours — agents work inside them, never around them
  • Anything unusual comes back to a person automatically
  • Nothing reaches a channel until it's been checked

What "one source of truth" actually means

Not one place to store data. One version everyone can trust.

Validated continuously

Checked as it changes, not in quarterly QA. Errors caught before a channel ever sees them.

Traceable end to end

Every value carries its lineage — where it came from, when it changed, who or what changed it.

Consistent everywhere

One product, read the same way by every person, channel and agent. No drift between systems.

The new stakes

When agents act at machine speed, bad data does too.

Before agents, a person looked at a record before it shipped. A wrong price or mismatched spec got caught at that checkpoint. Agents publishing hundreds of records an hour don't stop to look. By the time the mistake is spotted, it's already live on every marketplace, every sales channel, and every AI answer engine that pulled from your feed.

One bad value
A single wrong attribute
Every marketplace listing
Every sales channel
Every AI answer engine

Get the data right and speed is your advantage. Get it wrong and speed is the problem.

The decision you're accountable for

You can't let an agent act on data you can't vouch for.

An agent making thousands of decisions a day is only as safe as the data underneath it. To stand behind every call it makes on your behalf, you need to prove where each value came from and that it was right — not just trust that it was.

That proof used to be a nice-to-have. Now the regulators require it.

Regulatory readiness

EU AI Act and EU DPP both demand provenance and traceability most platforms can't show. Here it's designed in — part of the product, not bought as a separate services engagement.

  • Full data lineage and audit trail across every human and agent action
  • Provenance on each value — where it came from, who or what changed it, when
  • Compliance shipped as a product feature, not a professional-services line

The shift

AI agents are the new brand discovery layer.

The brands they cite are the ones structured for them to find. Most are not. For years, being found meant ranking in search and showing up on the channels you control. That's changing — fast.

AI referral traffic is already up 393% year on year. The shift isn't ahead of you — it's underway.

How discovery used to work
Customers searched.
They landed on your site, your listing, your category page. You could see them arrive, measure them, and optimise for them.
How discovery works now
Customers ask an AI.
It reads product data, picks what to recommend, and answers. The customer acts on it — and never visits, never shows up in your analytics.

If you're in the answer, you win a sale you didn't chase. If you're not, you lose one you'll never see.

Why engines skip products

Agents can't recommend what they can't read.

An engine recommends what it can confidently understand: complete, consistent, structured, machine-readable data. When yours is thin or inconsistent, it does the rational thing — skips your product for one it can trust.

Being citable isn't a marketing problem anymore. It's a product-data problem.

What it actually takes to get cited:

Complete & consistent

No gaps, no contradictions across sources. The same product reads the same way everywhere.

Machine-readable by design

Structured attributes — not copy buried in formats built for human eyes.

GEO / AEO-native

The data model the answer engines reward when they choose what to cite.

What it means for the business

A discovery channel that compounds.

  • Win share of voice in AI surfaces
    Show up in the answers where customers now decide. Citation visibility across ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity and Google AI Overviews — the share of voice you currently can't see in your dashboard.
  • Protect the brand at machine speed
    The AI describes your product from your data — not a competitor's spec or a stale third-party listing. Brand voice preserved across every agent surface.
  • As performance marketing erodes, the gap widens
    Discovery is shifting from paid funnels to AI-mediated answers. The products structured for citation pull ahead — and the distance grows for everyone still optimising only for the old channels.

GEO and AEO, in plain terms

Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO)

Making your products readable and recommendable by generative AI — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini.

Answer Engine Optimisation (AEO)

The same discipline, for answer engines like Perplexity.

Both come down to one thing: structured, trustworthy, machine-readable product data. The platform that operates on that kind of data is the platform that produces it.
