Cost per SKU is where the architecture turns into money.

It's the fully-loaded annual cost of running the product information function, divided by the products you actively sell: team, tools, services and integration, all in. Four lines make up the number, and they don't behave the same way.

The team line is the largest — typically 55 to 65% — and the one that scales almost linearly with the catalogue. A wider range, more channels and more markets means more people doing manual data work. Past a certain volume that stops being a staffing question and becomes a ceiling on how much you can profitably sell.

Most Tier 1 distributors sit between €12 and €20 per SKU today — and the figure climbs every quarter, as channels, agents and compliance demands keep adding to the four lines.

This is architecture, not a discount.

The saving doesn't come from cheaper licensing. It comes from the platform being API-only with full UI/API parity and 700+ task-level endpoints, so agents can do the operational work a team used to do by hand. A vendor that bolted AI onto a screen-first PIM can't reach the same cost structure without rebuilding the platform underneath it.
