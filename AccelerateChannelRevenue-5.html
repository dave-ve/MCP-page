What caps growth

What's actually limiting your channel growth?

Rarely demand — usually the cost and effort of running each new channel. That's the ceiling. When agents operate the catalogue, it lifts. Three limits fall away:

The cost ceiling

Normally each new channel earns less than the last, until one costs more to run than it makes. With agents that cost stays low, so that point moves far out.

The effort ceiling

Manual upkeep limits how many channels a team can run. Agents carry the load, so how many you operate stops being a question of hours.

The break-even ceiling

A small market has to clear a high bar to justify the setup. When setup is near-zero, the channels you used to skip become revenue worth taking.

The architecture

Why can't my current PIM fix this?

Most were built to be run through a screen, so the work scales with the people doing the clicking. Bolting on AI doesn't change that — the limit is the architecture, not the interface. One architectural shift lifts it, and commerce has already been through it once:

The part everyone did

Headless commerce

Splitting the storefront from the commerce engine, so any frontend can plug in. Proven, widespread — and only half the picture.

The part that's still missing

Headless, agent-first PIM

Your product data isn't locked behind a UI either — and it's built for agents, not just API calls. Your team, your channels and your AI agents all work from the same source, through whatever interface fits.

Headless commerce decoupled the storefront. We decoupled the work from the screen — so agents, not just people, can run the catalogue.

Reaching a new channel, market or language normally takes a wave of work — and that work, not the opportunity, is what usually caps how fast you can expand. Agents take it on; your team guides them and handles what needs a human eye.
